Explore the City of Boston's operating budget. This section provides insights into spending patterns across different departments and categories. Visualizations below showcase trends and discrepancies in operating expenditures over time.

Expenditures by Department

Overview: The pie chart above represents how Boston allocates its budget among major departments.

Insights:

  • Boston Public Schools receives the largest allocation, totaling $1,526,629,446, which is the dominant portion of the budget.
  • Other significant categories include the Police Department, Fire Department, Pensions, and Charter School Tuition.
  • From this Education and Public Safety seems to be the largest focus area for city investments.

Distribution of Expenses by Type

Overview: The pie chart above breaks down spending by category of expenses (e.g., salaries, services, supplies).

Insights:

  • Personnel Services (e.g., salaries, wages, and benefits) make up the largest portion, indicating the city's workforce is a significant expense.
  • Other Expenses and Contractual Services are the next largest spending categories.
  • Smaller allocations are made for Equipment, Supplies & Materials, and Fixed Expenses, which reflect operational and capital costs.

Detailed Spending Categories

Spending Trends Over Time (FY22-FY25)

Overview: The line graph tracks spending growth over fiscal years for major departments.

Insights:

  • Boston Public Schools shows a consistent upward trend in spending, reflecting increased investments in education.
  • Police Department, Pensions, and Charter School Tuition have more gradual increases, suggesting steady but slower growth in these areas.
  • Spending on the Fire Department remains relatively flat compared to other categories.

Comparison of Budget Categories over Time

Overview: The line graph compares spending growth for top 5 increased budget categories over fiscal years.

Insights:

  • Boston Public Schools continue to receive the most funding and shows the fastest growth.
  • Pensions and Debt Service show steady growth, reflecting the city's long-term financial commitments.
  • Charter School Tuition and Police Department budgets remain stable, with slight increases.

Spending Trends by Expense Type (FY22-FY25)

Overview: The line graph shows trends by expense types (e.g., personnel, equipment, services).

Insights:

  • Personnel Services consistently dominate spending and show steady growth, reflecting the cost of maintaining and increasing the workforce.
  • Current Charges & Obligations and Fixed Expenses also grow, indicating rising operational costs.
  • Smaller categories like Supplies & Materials and Equipment show little to no growth, suggesting limited investment in new purchases or upgrades.
